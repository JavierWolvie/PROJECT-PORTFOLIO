# INS ICT Integration Needs Assessment Report – 2025
# Project Overview

The Instant Network Schools (INS) ICT Integration Needs Assessment Report – 2025 provides insights into the current state of ICT integration in partner schools within Kakuma and Kalobeyei. The purpose of this report is to evaluate ICT resources, assess digital literacy among students and teachers, identify challenges, and provide actionable recommendations to enhance digital learning environments.

# Objectives

The assessment was conducted to:

Examine the availability and utilization of ICT resources in participating schools.

Evaluate digital literacy levels among students, teachers, and ICT coaches.

Identify key challenges affecting the sustainability of ICT-supported learning.

Offer practical recommendations to guide future program support and capacity-building efforts.

# Data Collection & Methodology

Survey Method: Structured questionnaires for administrators, teachers, ICT coaches, and students.

Data Cleaning: Ensured consistency, handled missing values, and standardized variables.

Analysis Tools: Microsoft Excel and Python (pandas and NumPy libraries) were used to process and analyze data.

Data Coding: Open-ended responses were categorized into themes for qualitative insights.

# Key Findings

Limited but growing ICT infrastructure, including laptops, projectors, and internet access.

Variable digital literacy among students and moderate proficiency among teachers and ICT coaches.

Challenges include inconsistent internet connectivity, lack of professional development, and insufficient technical support.

# Recommendations

Implement regular capacity-building programs for teachers and ICT coaches.

Invest in ICT infrastructure, including functional computer labs and reliable internet.

Establish technical support teams for equipment maintenance.

Integrate ICT tools effectively into the school curriculum.

Conduct monitoring and evaluation to measure progress and adapt strategies.
